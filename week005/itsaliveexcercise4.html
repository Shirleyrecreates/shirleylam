Things: A Story of the Sixties by George Perec

Chapter 1

Web Production 1 Page

It's Alive Excercise

There, life would be easy, simple. All the servitudes,all the problems brought by material existence would find a natural solution. A cleaning lady would come every morning. Every fortnight,
  • wine
  • oil
  • sugar
would be delivered.

There would be a huge bright kitchen with
  • blue tiles decorated with heraldic emblems
  • three china plates decorated with yellow arabesques in metallic paint
  • cupboards everywhere
  • a handsome whitewood table in the middle with stools and bench-seats.
It would be pleasant to come and sit there, every morning, after a shower, scarcely dressed. On the table there would be
  • a sizable stoneware butter dish
  • jars of marmalade
  • honey
  • toast
  • grapefruit cut in two
It would be early. It would be May, the start of a long summers day.They would
  1. open the mail
  2. open the newspapers
  3. light their first cigarette
  4. They would go out
Their work would keep them busy for a few hours only, in the morning. They would meet
  1. for lunch a sandwich or steak, according to their mood;
  2. they would have coffee at a street cafe
  3. then go home, on foot, slowly
Their flat would rarely be tidy, but its very untidiness would be its greatest charm.They would hardly bother themselves with it: they would live in it. The comfort of their surroundings would seem to them to be
  • an established fact
  • a datum
  • a state of their nature
Their attention would be elsewhere:
  • on the book they would open
  • on the text they would draft
  • on the record they would listen to
  • on their dialogue engaged afresh each day
They would work for a long while. Then they would
  1. dine or go out for a dinner
  2. see old friends
  3. they would walk together
Sometimes it would seem to them
that a whole life could be led harmoniously
between these book-lined walls,
amongst these objects so perfectly domesticated


that they would have ended up believing
these bright, soft, simple and beautiful things
had only ever been made for their sole use.


But they would not feel enslaved by them:
on some days,they would go off on a chance adventure.
No plan seemed impossible to them.


They would not know rancour, or bitterness or envy.


For their means and desires would always match in all ways.
They would call this balance happiness and,
with their freedom,with their wisdom and their culture,
they would know how to retain
and to reveal it in every moment of their living,

together.
